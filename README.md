## Abstract

The standard $\Lambda$CDM model relies on several fine-tuned or problematic pillars, including the cosmic singularity, the ad-hoc inflaton field, and a strictly constant dark energy component ($\Lambda$). Crucially, it faces the modern "Hubble Tension" crisis, where late-universe observations ($H_0 \approx 73$ km/s/Mpc) systematically diverge from early-universe Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) inferences ($H_0 \approx 67.4$ km/s/Mpc). In this paper, we establish a novel, non-singular black hole cosmology based on three core principles: (i) our universe resides inside a parent black hole expanding due to external accretion; (ii) the constancy of the speed of light emerges naturally from a Painlevé-Gullstrand fluid coordinate mapping; and (iii) a rigid Planckian density threshold induces a gravitational bounce ($\mathcal{F}(\rho) \to 0$), removing the Big Bang singularity. The model yields a positive accretion coupling constant $\eta \approx 2.3 \times 10^{-6}$, naturally resolving the Hubble tension without invoking dark energy or inflation.

## Key Results

- **Hubble Tension Resolved:** Local $H_0 = 72.4 \pm 0.8$ km/s/Mpc vs CMB $H_0 = 67.4$ km/s/Mpc.
- **Singularity-Free:** Planck density bounce via $\mathcal{F}(\rho) = 1 - \rho^2/\rho_P^2$.
- **No Dark Energy:** Late-time acceleration driven by external accretion with $\eta = 2.3 \times 10^{-6}$.
- **KBC Void Consistency:** BHC model predicts density contrast $\delta \approx 0.46$ at $z \approx 0.055$, matching the KBC Void observation ($\delta = 0.46 \pm 0.06$), while $\Lambda$CDM is excluded at 6.04$\sigma$.
